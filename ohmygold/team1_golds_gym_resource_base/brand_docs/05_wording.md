## 2. Language Style Rules

### French Register: Informal "Tu"
Gold's Gym France exclusively uses the **informal "tu" form** (not "vous"), creating immediate familiarity:

> "Tu veux pas juste t'entrainer. Tu veux faire partie de la legende."

### Sentence Structure
- **Short, punchy sentences** -- Avoid complex subordinate clauses
- **Imperative mood** -- Commands that inspire action
- **Rhetorical questions** -- Engage the reader directly
- **Fragment sentences** -- For dramatic emphasis

### Verb Tense Preferences
- **Present tense** for descriptions and current state
- **Imperative** for calls-to-action
- **Future** for aspirational statements
- **Past tense** only for historical/heritage references

## 6. Writing Patterns & Structures

### Three-Part Lists (Tricolon)
Gold's Gym frequently uses rhythmic three-part structures:

> *"Une vraie salle. Du vrai matos. De la vraie sueur."*
>
> *"Puissance, agilite, mental d'acier."*

### Contrast Structures
Setting up opposition for dramatic effect:

> *"Pas un simple abonnement. Une vraie salle."*
>
> *"Tu veux pas juste t'entrainer. Tu veux faire partie de la legende."*

### Heritage-to-Present Bridges
Connecting 60 years of history to today:

> *"Au depart simple point de rencontre de la scene, aujourd'hui The Mecca du mouvement mondial du fitness."*

### Direct Address + Challenge
Speaking directly to the reader with a challenge:

> *"Debutant ou expert, viens t'eprouver et repousser tes limites."*
>
> *"Que tu sois debutant ou veteran, c'est ici que tu construis ta legende."*

## 8. Content Formatting Standards

### Headlines
- ALL CAPS for section headers (e.g., "GOLD'S IRON GROUND")
- Title case for sub-headers
- Bold for emphasis within text

### Feature Lists
- Use checkmarks (emoji or text) for benefits
- Short phrases, not full sentences
- Consistent structure across tiers

### Pricing Display
- Large font for price amount
- Small "EUR" or "/semaine" suffix
- Strikethrough for original prices during promotions

## 12. Key Content Insights for App Design

1. **Always lead with legacy** -- Open with heritage, close with action
2. **Use "tu" everywhere** -- Never formal "vous" in user-facing copy
3. **Arena metaphors work** -- Frame workouts as battles, victories
4. **Three-part rhythm** -- "X, Y, Z" structures are brand DNA
5. **French + English mix** -- Certain English terms are brand-embedded
6. **Emoji for energy** -- Fire, muscle, lightning for intensity
7. **Every member is a champion** -- Inclusive empowerment messaging
8. **Founder member exclusivity** -- Reference being "first" frequently
