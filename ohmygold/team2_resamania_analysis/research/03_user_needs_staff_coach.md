**Research Date:** April 2026
**Analyst Confidence Level:** High (multiple corroborating sources)

## 3.1 Who Are Staff & Coaches?

This persona group includes:
- **Front desk staff** — checking in members, processing payments, answering questions
- **Fitness coaches / personal trainers** — delivering sessions, managing client programs
- **Group fitness instructors** — teaching classes, managing attendance
- **Facility maintenance staff** — equipment checks, cleaning, reporting issues
- **Sales staff** — lead follow-up, tours, membership sales [^59^] [^58^]

## 3.2 Staff Daily Workflow & Pain Points

### 3.2.1 Front Desk Staff Workflows

Front desk staff are the operational backbone of any gym. Their daily software needs include [^59^] [^68^]:

| Task | Current Friction | Ideal Solution |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Member check-in | Manual paper lists or clunky systems | QR code or biometric scan with instant verification [^59^] |
| New member signup | Paper forms, data entry | Digital signup with waiver integration [^42^] |
| Class booking assistance | Multiple systems | Single interface for real-time booking [^68^] |
| Payment processing | Separate POS terminal | Integrated payment in same system [^80^] |
| Answering member questions | Limited visibility into member history | Centralized member profile with full history [^42^] |
| Equipment issue reporting | Phone calls, paper logs | Mobile photo reporting with ticket creation [^40^] |

### 3.2.2 Coach & Trainer Workflows

Fitness coaches face a unique challenge: **admin work scales with client count**. Trainers who devote **more than 30% of their time to admin** commonly see slower growth and lower client satisfaction [^53^]. The tipping point typically comes when moving from 5 clients to 30+ active clients [^58^].

**Daily coach workflow:**
1. Review client schedules and prepare sessions
2. Deliver training (in-person or virtual)
3. Log client progress and workout results
4. Communicate with clients (feedback, motivation, adjustments)
5. Manage own schedule and availability
6. Handle billing and payment follow-ups [^58^] [^61^]

### 3.2.3 Top Pain Points for Staff & Coaches

1. **Excessive administrative burden** — scheduling, billing, and constant client messages eat into coaching time [^53^]
2. **Fragmented tools** — workout plans in one app, scheduling in another, communication in WhatsApp/email [^58^]
3. **No-shows and late cancellations** — without automated reminders, coaches lose revenue [^58^]
4. **Client progress tracking** — difficult to maintain consistent records across multiple clients [^63^]
5. **Schedule coordination** — managing 30+ clients across varying schedules is unsustainable manually [^58^]
6. **Limited mobile access** — many systems are desktop-only, preventing floor staff from accessing tools [^84^]

## 3.3 Must-Have Features for Staff

### 3.3.1 Check-In & Attendance Management
- **QR code scanning** for fast member check-in [^54^]
- **Biometric fingerprint check-in** for both members and staff [^59^]
- Real-time attendance dashboard [^59^]
- Automatic attendance logging linked to member profiles [^52^]
- Kiosk mode for self check-in at front desk [^87^]

### 3.3.2 Mobile Staff App
Staff need a mobile app that enables [^84^] [^59^]:
- Schedule viewing and management from anywhere
- Member lookup and profile access
- Push notifications for important announcements
- Photo-based equipment issue reporting
- Real-time booking approval
- In-app messaging with members and other staff
- Dark mode for comfortable all-day use [^59^]

### 3.3.3 Role-Based Access
- Custom roles controlling permissions per staff type [^59^]
- Owner (full access) + designated staff roles [^59^]
- Staff should only see functions relevant to their job [^62^]
- Secure data access tailored to responsibility level [^54^]

### 3.3.4 Equipment & Facility Reporting
- Photo-based incident/maintenance reporting
- Work order creation and tracking [^40^]
- Daily checklist completion (opening/closing tasks, cleaning) [^40^]
- Assignment hub for delegating tasks [^59^]

## 3.4 Must-Have Features for Coaches

### 3.4.1 Client Management
A good coaching app must centralize all client information [^58^] [^63^]:
- Client profiles with health and fitness data
- Progress tracking and check-ins with photos/metrics
- In-app messaging or voice notes
- Workout history and attendance
- Habit coaching features (water intake, daily habits) [^58^]
- Goal tracking and milestone recognition [^57^]

### 3.4.2 Workout Programming & Delivery
- Large exercise library with video demonstrations [^58^]
- Templates for workout programming
- Custom training plan creation [^58^]

### 3.4.3 Scheduling & Booking

## 3.6 What Makes Coaching Software Great

Based on industry analysis, the best coaching platforms share these traits [^58^] [^63^] [^67^]:

1. **All-in-one architecture** — workout programming, client management, nutrition, payments, and communication in one system
2. **White-label / branded app option** — clients interact with the coach's brand, not a third-party platform [^58^]
3. **Mobile-first design** — both coach and client must have excellent mobile experiences [^67^]
4. **Automation** — scheduling reminders, payment collection, progress check-ins happen automatically
5. **Scalability** — pricing should not punish coaches as they grow their client list [^58^]
