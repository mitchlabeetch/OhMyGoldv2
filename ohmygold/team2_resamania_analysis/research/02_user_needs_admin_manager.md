# Dimension 2: User Needs by Role — Admin & Manager

**Research Date:** April 2026
**Analyst Confidence Level:** High (multiple corroborating sources)

## 2.1 Who Are Admins and Managers?

In the gym/fitness context, "admins" and "managers" span a spectrum of roles:
- **Independent gym owners** running single-location businesses
- **Franchise operators** managing 5-50+ locations
- **Club managers** handling day-to-day operations
- **Regional directors** overseeing multi-site performance
- **Operations managers** focused on staffing, scheduling, and facilities [^64^] [^62^]

---

## 2.2 Biggest Pain Points

### 2.2.1 System Fragmentation
The most-cited pain point across all sources is **disconnected systems**. Multiple logins, manual reconciliations, reporting blind spots, billing errors, and staff constantly switching screens create "operational chaos" [^64^]. As one analysis put it: "When you've got one system for billing, another for scheduling, and spreadsheets for everything else, you spend more time controlling the chaos than managing your gym" [^68^].

### 2.2.2 Manual Billing and Payment Follow-ups
- Chasing failed payments, expired cards, and overdue invoices consumes enormous admin time [^79^]
- Manual invoicing and payment tracking with automated systems drives up to **25% revenue increases** when automated [^52^]
- Proration logic for membership upgrades/downgrades creates constant confusion [^80^]

### 2.2.3 Retention Blindness
Gym owners typically don't know a member is at risk until they submit a cancellation request. Without predictive tools, retention efforts are purely reactive [^79^] [^80^]. AI-powered systems that flag members with **50% attendance drop over 30 days** and trigger automatic outreach are game-changers [^80^].

### 2.2.4 Multi-Location Complexity
For franchise and multi-location operators:
- Maintaining brand consistency across locations is difficult [^66^]
- Financial controls for royalty collection are cumbersome [^66^]
- No centralized visibility — data lives in separate systems per location [^82^]
- Standardizing class formats, pricing, and promotions requires manual oversight [^66^]

### 2.2.5 Reporting Delays
Waiting until end-of-month to review performance is "outdated" [^84^]. Managers need real-time dashboards for revenue, attendance, and retention metrics [^80^]. Spreadsheet-based reporting is still common and error-prone [^62^].

### 2.2.6 Lead Response Time
Response time directly affects conversion rates. Manual lead follow-up means leads go cold before staff can respond [^79^]. "Leads cannot get lost thanks to the safety net with automated follow-up calls" [^62^].

## 2.4 Daily Workflow Needs

A typical club manager's day involves [^62^] [^64^] [^68^]:

1. **Morning (opening):**
   - Review overnight attendance and revenue reports
   - Check staff schedules and any no-shows
   - Review at-risk member alerts
   - Approve any pending membership changes or refunds

2. **Midday (operations):**
   - Monitor class bookings and waitlists
   - Respond to lead inquiries (or review automated follow-ups)
   - Handle equipment maintenance work orders [^40^]
   - Review staff performance metrics

3. **Evening (closing/analysis):**
   - Review daily performance dashboard
   - Plan next day's staffing
   - Follow up on retention alerts
   - Review financial reports and billing status

## 2.5 What Franchise Operators Specifically Need

Franchise operators have unique requirements beyond single-location managers [^66^]:

1. **Centralized franchise control panel** — governance over key operations including membership pricing thresholds, promotional campaign approval, standardized class formats, branding control [^66^]
2. **Unified reporting and analytics** — real-time dashboards aggregating data from every location [^66^]
3. **Marketing template distribution** — approved templates for franchisees with local customization options [^66^]
4. **International expansion support** — multiple currencies, languages, and international payment processing [^66^]
5. **Compliance tracking** — ensuring all locations meet brand and regulatory standards [^66^]

## 2.6 Decision Criteria for Software Selection

When evaluating gym management software, admins and managers prioritize [^42^] [^44^] [^68^]:

1. **Ease of use** — "Your software should be intuitive enough that your staff adopts it" [^42^]
2. **Pricing transparency** — hidden costs and required sales calls are major friction points [^42^]
3. **Scalability** — ability to handle more members and additional locations [^42^]
4. **Integration capabilities** — wearables, accounting software, access control, marketing automation [^38^]
5. **Customer support quality** — responsive human support is "non-negotiable" [^49^]
6. **Free trial availability** — "never skip this step" [^38^]
7. **AI/automation capabilities** — increasingly a competitive differentiator [^79^]
